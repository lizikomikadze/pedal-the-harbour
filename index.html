How to rent a bike

Choose local rentals for service and fit, or dockless for quick hops.

Local rental shops

Best for comfort and safety: correct frame size, brakes check, helmet availability, and route advice. Great if you’re doing the full day.

  • Ask for: helmet, lights, bell, phone holder.
  • Check: brake bite, tire pressure, seat height, chain.
  • Bring: ID (may be required), water, sunscreen.

HelloRide (dockless)

Convenient for short segments if bikes are available nearby. A good option if you want flexibility or you’re meeting friends mid‑route.

  • Pros: quick pickup/return, no shop hours.
  • Watch: bike condition varies—do a quick safety check.
  • Be mindful: park neatly and avoid blocking footpaths.

Sustainability tips & cycling safety

Small habits make the ride safer and the route cleaner.

Low‑waste riding

  • Bring: reusable bottle + a small tote for snacks.
  • Choose: dine‑in when possible; decline disposable cutlery.
  • Refill: top up water at stops instead of buying new bottles.
  • Leave no trace: keep waterfronts and paths litter‑free.

Basic cycling safety

  • Helmet + lights: especially if riding near sunset.
  • Keep left: follow cycle track rules and be predictable.
  • Signal: use hand signals; ring bell gently to pass.
  • Hydrate: heat can sneak up—take short shade breaks.
